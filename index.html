Pros of living in Oklahoma City

Are you planning on moving to Oklahoma? If only you had thought of it sooner! Back in 1889, during the Oklahoma Land Rush, one had the option to claim unoccupied land up to 160 acres…for free! But since it’s a bit late now, let’s focus on your options today. Oklahoma ranks 28th in terms of its population density and 20th in terms of land mass. What this means is that there’s still plenty of room for new settlers, although the Sooner state’s population has been growing steadily over the last few years. The state is nicknamed ‘Sooner’ because many people landed here sooner than the official start of the Land Rush to grab the best land. There are plenty of more fun facts and trivia, which we will discuss here in addition to the pros and cons of moving to Oklahoma.

Living Conditions

Is it too hot??

In Oklahoma City, the summers are hot and muggy; the winters are very cold, snowy, and windy; and it is partly cloudy year round. Over the course of the year, the temperature typically varies from 30°F to 94°F and is rarely below 17°F or above 102°F.The hot season lasts for 3.3 months, from June 5 to September 15, with an average daily high temperature above 85°F. The hottest month of the year in Oklahoma City is July, with an average high of 93°F and low of 73°F.

Are there housing issues?

Housing market summary

In July 2022, Oklahoma City home prices were up 14.3% compared to last year, selling for a median price of $263K. On average, homes in Oklahoma City sell after 7 days on the market compared to 5 days last year. There were 1,024 homes sold in July this year, down from 1,149 last year.The Oklahoma City housing market is very competitive. Homes in Oklahoma City receive 4 offers on average and sell in around 5 days. The average sale price of a home in Oklahoma City was $263K last month, up 14.3% since last year. The average sale price per square foot in Oklahoma City is $150, up 16.3% since last year.
